History of T20

The inaugral of the T20 World Cup was in 2007 in South Africa. India won their Very First T20 World Cup in 2007.

During early 2000s the Benson & Hedges Cup ended so the England Cricket Board(ECB) needed a new format. They settled on a 20-over format. Many of them were in favour of the format. Thus this format came. The first official Twenty20 matches were played on 13 June 2003 between the English counties in the Twenty20 Cup. The first season of Twenty20 in England was a relative success. The first Twenty20 match held at Lord's, on 15 July 2004 between Middlesex and Surrey, attracted a crowd of 27,509, the largest attendance for any county cricket game at the ground other than a one-day final since 1953.
